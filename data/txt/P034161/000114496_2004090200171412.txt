                  AGREEMENT AMENDING LOAN AGREEMENT
        AGREEMENT, dated June 23, 1995, between NACIONAL
FINANCIERA, S.N.C. (the Borrower) and INTERNATIONAL BANK FOR
RECONSTRUCTION AND DEVELOPMENT (the Bank).
        WHEREAS (A) the Borrower and the Bank have entered into a
Loan Agreement (Financial Sector Technical Assistance Project)
dated March 9, 1995 (the Loan Agreement) for the purpose of
assisting in the financing of the project described in Schedule
2 to the Loan Agreement (the Project) and United Mexican States
(the Guarantor) and the Bank have entered into a Guarantee
Agreement (Financial Sector Technical Assistance Project) of
even date with the Loan Agreement (the Guarantee Agreement) for
the execution of the Project and the guarantee of the
obligations of the Borrower under the Loan Agreement;
        (B)     the Borrower has requested the Bank to provide
additional assistance towards the financing of the Project by
increasing the amount made available under the Loan Agreement
by an amount in various currencies equivalent to thirteen
million eight hundred thousand dollars ($13,800,000);
        (C)     by an agreement (the Amending Guarantee Agreement) of
even date herewith between the Guarantor and the Bank, the
Guarantor has agreed to guarantee such additional obligations
Page  2
of the Borrower and to undertake such other obligations as set
forth in the Amending Guarantee Agreement; and
        WHEREAS the Bank has agreed, on the basis, inter alia, of
the foregoing, to provide such additional assistance to the
Borrower upon the terms and conditions set forth in this
Agreement;
        NOW THEREFORE the parties hereto hereby agree as follows:

                            ARTICLE II
                   Effective Date; Termination
        Section 2.01. This Amending Loan Agreement shall not
become effective until evidence satisfactory to the Bank shall
have been furnished to the Bank: (a) that the execution and
delivery of this Amending Loan Agreement on behalf of the
Borrower have been duly authorized or ratified by all necessary
corporate action; and (b) that the execution and delivery of
the Amending Guarantee Agreement on behalf of the Guarantor
Page  4
have been duly authorized or ratified by all necessary
governmental action.
        Section 2.02. As part of the evidence to be furnished pur-
suant to Section 2.01 of this Amending Loan Agreement, there
shall be furnished to the Bank an opinion or opinions
satisfactory to the Bank of counsel acceptable to the Bank
showing, on behalf of the Borrower and the Guarantor, that the
Amending Loan Agreement and the Amending Guarantee Agreement
have been duly authorized or ratified by, and executed and
delivered on behalf of, respectively, the Borrower and the
Guarantor and are legally binding upon, respectively, the
Borrower and the Guarantor in accordance with their terms.
        Section 2.03. This Amending Loan Agreement shall come into
force and effect on the date upon which the Bank dispatches to
the Borrower notice of its acceptance of the evidence required
by Section 2.01 of this Amending Loan Agreement.
        Section 2.04. If this Amending Loan Agreement shall not
have come into force and effect by September 25, 1995 this
Amending Loan Agreement and all obligations of the parties
hereunder shall terminate, unless the Bank establishes a later
date for the purposes of this Section. If this Amending Loan
Agreement shall terminate under the provisions of this Section,
the Loan Agreement shall continue in full force and effect, as
if this Amending Loan Agreement had not been executed.

                            SCHEDULE 2
                    Description of the Project
        The objectives of the Project are: (a) to improve the
safety and soundness of the financial system through improved
prudential regulation and supervision, and through greater
incentives for self-policing by market participants; (b) to
support the development of the pension system; and (c) to
improve public investment evaluation and budgeting.
        The Project consists of the following parts, subject to
such modifications thereof as the Borrower and the Bank may
agree upon from time to time to achieve such objectives:
Part A:       Financial Sector Regulation
        Provision of coordinated technical assistance through SHCP
with respect to:
1.      CNBV
        (a)   Banks and Financial Groups:
              Analysis and improvement of: authorization policies
and procedures; regulatory and supervision policies; regulatory
accounting principles; supervision processes (both in situ and
extra situ) and consolidated supervision of financial
conglomerates; modernization of the information systems; and
institutionalization of human resource development.
        (b)   Securities Markets and Brokerage Firms:
              Analysis and improvement of: the regulatory and
supervisory policy framework of the organized securities
markets and brokerage firms; self-regulation; and the legal,
regulatory and supervision policy framework for new financial
instruments such as, for example, derivatives.
        (c)   Support for the execution of the Program:
              Support for the execution of the Program including,
inter alia, technical assistance with respect to the
Guarantor's accelerated program of bank inspections, diagnostic
studies of banks, selection of advisors for bank
restructurings, a study of appraisal standards, review of
impediments to securitization of bank and non-bank originated
assets, the UDI-scheme, a study of the legal framework for
secured transactions, and a study of the obstacles to voluntary
debt restructuring (including recognition, execution and
enforcement of out-of-court workouts and arbitration awards).
2.      CNSF
        Analysis and improvement of: supervision processes;
regulatory and supervisory policies applicable to insurance
companies, insurance brokers, adjusters and reinsurance
companies; CNSF's capacity to evaluate actuarial audits;
certification procedures for actuaries; and regulatory
reporting standards and accounting principles consistent with
those adopted in the other Commissions.
3.      CONSAR
Page  6
        (a)    Regulatory Framework:
               The establishment of a regulatory framework for the
private investment management of SAR accounts, the development
of a consistent regulatory framework for voluntary occupational
retirement plans offered by private sector and parastatal
enterprises, the development of CONSAR's capacity to supervise
and inspect the SAR accounts, the design of a public
communications program in support of pension reform, and
preparation of a human resource development program.
        (b)    Annuities Market Study:
              Execution of a study by CONSAR, jointly with CNSF, of
the annuities market and its implications for its further
development as the SAR matures, with emphasis on international
experience.
Part B:        Public Investment Evaluation and Budgeting
        Provision of technical assistance to SHCP's unit
responsible for public investment evaluation and budgeting with
respect to: inter-institutional coordination; establishment of
evaluation standards, dissemination of these standards and
training in project evaluation; evaluation of sector- and
region-specific public investment projects; establishment of a
national inventory of public investment projects and a system
for ranking and prioritization within and across economic
sectors; multi-annual budgeting; and ex-post monitoring and
evaluation.
