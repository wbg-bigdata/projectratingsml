                         Project Development Objectives
                         Project Development Objective (from Project Appraisal Document)

                         To contribute to Burundi's post-war revival by restoring part of the priority road network, generating employment for the rural poor and improving institutional capacity
                         in the road sector

                         Implementation Status Overview
                         Overall, implementation is progressing well and the PDO is likely to be achieved by the extended project closing date of July 31, 2014. More specifically:




                                                                                                                                                                                                          Page 1 of 10
                             The World Bank                                                                                                                                     Report No: ISR14489



                         a) The execution of the annual project work plan and budget for 2014 has reached about 35 percent, of the total amount planned for the calendar year 2014.
                         b) The disbursement rate for the Additional Financing (AF) has reached US$10.9 million (57.5 percent) as of April 8, 2014, while contractual commitments have reached about 100
                         percent. It is expected that the disbursements will reach 61 percent after taking into account the works invoices already approved and under processing for payment by end of April
                         2014.
Public Disclosure Copy




                         c) The implementation of the balance of works funded under the project is proceeding in a satisfactory manner.
                         d) The support to OdR’s accounting and financial section for the compilation of its financial statements for the last 10 years, including the updating of the financial management
                         software as well as staff training is completed.
                         e) The contract for the assessment of the multi-year (5 years) road maintenance contracts was signed on February 06, 2014 for a duration of 2.25 months. The Consultant will review
                         the existing processing for the establishment and implementation of the road maintenance programs and make recommendations on how to improve the entire road maintenance
                         system in order to ensure efficiency in the road maintenance expenses funded by the National Road Maintenance Funds (FRN). The sample of the bidding documents for road
                         maintenance contracts will also be updated.
