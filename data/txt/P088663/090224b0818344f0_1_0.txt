                         Project Development Objectives
                         Project Development Objective (from Project Appraisal Document)

                         The objectives of the Project are to (i) expand and enhance the family medicine model of primary health care; (ii) build managementcapacity in the sector; and (iii)
                         strengthen the policy making process through the development and implementation of a system for monitoring and evaluating sector performance.

                         Implementation Status Overview
                         In FBiH, significant progress has been made in all components.
                         In Primary Health Care Restructuring:
                         The Program of Additional Training in Family Medicine (PAT) has continued in the four community health centers (Mostar, Sarajevo, Tuzla, and Zenica). During the 2011/2012
                         academic year, PAT was successfully completed by 56 physicians and 129 nurses who had to undergo a final examination at the end of training. Additional 70 physicians and 120
                         nurses will attend during the 2012/2013 academic year.

                         The pilots to implement pay-for-performance payment for preventive and promotional activities will start on January 1, 2013. The Technical Manual was finalized and 8 training
                         workshops were carried out.
                         The refurbishment and purchase of equipment for Family Medicine ambulantas (PHC clinics)– Phase 4 is under implementation. Contracts has been signed for the rehabilitation
                         works for 42 selected Family Medicine ambulantas. The procurement process for medical equipment and furniture for 50 selected Family Medicine ambulantas was launched.
Public Disclosure Copy




                         In Health Policy Formulation and Project Support. Contract with the Public Health Institute and external expert, and for the equipment to carry out the survey of the population’s health
                         status, were signed and preparation works are already under execution.

                         In RS,
                         in Primary Health Care Restructuring:. All works were contracted and being implemented (5 contracts for rehabilitation of 15 ambulantas in Modrica, Banjica, Magnojevici, and
                         Lopare). The FM teams and ambulantas are receiving support and training to strengthen their standards before the accreditation process is reinitiated. A new software application to
                         replace the version currently used by FM teams is ready to be installed.
                         In improvement of Health Management Capacity. The contract with the Public Health Institute’s Health Management Center was signed and the agreed training program is being
                         implemented as planned.
                         In Health Policy Formulation and Project Support. The outline for the new RS PHC strategy is ready and likely to be adopted during first half of 2013
