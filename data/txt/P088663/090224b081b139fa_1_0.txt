                         Project Development Objectives
                         Project Development Objective (from Project Appraisal Document)

                         The objectives of the Project are to (i) expand and enhance the family medicine model of primary health care; (ii) build managementcapacity in the sector; and (iii)
                         strengthen the policy making process through the development and implementation of a system for monitoring and evaluating sector performance.

                         Implementation Status Overview
                         This ISR reflects the findings of the implementation support visit conducted from March 18 to 22, 2013. All activities planned under the Additional Financing (AF), 15 months after its
                         effectiveness on December 27, 2011, are currently being implemented by both entities. Most Project Development Objective indicators are on track.

                         As previously reported, in Federation of Bosnia and Herzegovina (FBiH), significant progress has been made in all components.
                         In Component 1 – Primary Health Care Restructuring:


                                                                                                                                                                                                          Page 1 of 9

                         Program of Additional Training (PAT) in Family Medicine is being provided in accordance with the contracts executed with community health centers in Mostar, Sarajevo, Tuzla, and
                         Zenica. The pilot to implement pay-for-performance payment for preventive and promotional activities started on January 1, 2013 in Sarajevo, Tuzla, and Zenica. Contracts for this
                         pilot were signed with 50 teams in the intervention group and 50 teams in the control group. A specific software has been developed to report on and monitor the teams’ activities.
                         A total of 35 additional Family Medicine ambulantas (Primary Health Care (PHC) clinics) have been refurbished and equipped.
Public Disclosure Copy




                         The main objective of the IT component is the implementation of a nationally certified information system in all community health care centers of the F BiH (78 health care centers).

                         In Component 2 – Health Sector Management Capacity Development. Teaching aids and IT equipment have been procured for Sarajevo and Mostar.
                         In Component 3 – Health Policy Formulation and Project Support. The field work for the survey of the population’s health status and risk factor for Non-Communicable Diseases
                         (NCDs) is completed and the final report should be ready by September.

                         In Republica Sprska (RS), activities are also on track in accordance with the updated Procurement Plan.
                         In Component 1. All works under IDA financing are completed or under implementation.
                         51 Family Medicine (FM) Health Team has been accredited and another 50 are in the process of being accredited.
                         In Component 2. The Public Health Institute’s Health Management Center is mplementing the training program as planned.
                         In Component 3. The new RS PHC strategy was updated and is part of the overall RS Strategy comprised of all sectors and Line Ministries’ sub-strategies.
