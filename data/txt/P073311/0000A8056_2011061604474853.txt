                         Implementation Status Overview
                         As the project#s closing date approaches, good progress is being achieved towards the project's development objective of fulfilling Cambodia#s Millennium Development Goals
                         (MDGs) in water supply and sanitation. Eleven water supply facilities were built through Design-Build-Lease (DBL) and Output-Based Aid (OBA) contracts, and those facilities are

                                                                                                                                                                                                            Page 1 of 5

                         serving over 15,000 households in four project provinces. Already some 11,720 poor families on the outskirts of Phnom Penh have been connected to clean water supply from the
                         Phnom Penh Water Supply Authority (PPWSA), and additional works are now being completed to bring the total number of poor households with clean water up to the revised target

                         of 15,200. Some 1,075 poor households had access to improved sanitation situation through the pilot program of family latrine installation, and works are under way to achieve the
                         revised target of installing 7,147 latrines. PPWSA has met its operational performance targets with a working ratio of 0.39 (target=under 0.50) and a debt service ratio at 3.18
                         (target=greater than 1.3). Surveys are underway to assess beneficiary satisfaction with the works performed under the project, but recent ad hoc interviews revealed very positive
                         results.
