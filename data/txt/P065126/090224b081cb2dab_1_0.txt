                         Implementation Status Overview
                         The restructuring of the project has been approved by the World Bank and the amendments to the legal agreement countersigned by the Government of Guinea since March 20th,
                         2012. The goals of the restructuring were to: (i) revise the PDOs; (ii) modify the institutional arrangements; (iii) revise disbursement categories; and (iv) extend the closing date to
                         December 30th, 2013. The project picked up disbursements on March 30th, 2012. The project action plan for 2013 has been finalized and approved. So far the project strengthened
                         the flow of care for at-risk deliveries, improved the norms and adherence to the norms for basic as well as emergency birth delivery in public health centers; improved emergency birth
                         delivery care at all levels of health centers trough the project zone, Improved IMCI activities ; project ensured a steady supply of drugs, vaccines, bednets, equipment. Purchased
                         ambulances, supervision vehicles, motorcycles and bicycles; the interventions consisted also in training of nurses, midwives, Doctors in the field but also in training people at central
                         level in maternal and child health, the project also invested in blood collection and security. The project improved access to care by improving the referral system via radio and

                         studies on human resources, the project also financed the recruitment of 490 nurses to address the shortage of midwives in the project zone. Regarding the health services quality,
                         an emphasis has been put on nosocomial infections prevention in health centers. For MURIGAS (community pooled insurance) since all health matters related to pregnancy became
                         free, the project organized a health forum on mutual and signed contracts with 4 NGOs specialized in Mutual to create or reactivated the old health mutuals. On the institutional side

                         •         There was a lot of efforts put into revamping the health information system from training, to recruitment of technicians, to purchasing of equipment (hardware and software).

                         been trained and equipped with the necessary equipment at all levels of the health system, in hospitals at the prefecture level, health centers at the village level, and health posts in
                         rural areas.
