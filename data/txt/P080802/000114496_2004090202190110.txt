SCHEDULE 2 
 
Description of the Project 
 
The objectives of the Project are to assist the Borrower in: (a) relieving social and 
economic hardships caused by the Earthquakes through the reconstruction of damaged 
public and private assets in the Project Area; and (b) developing an effective monitoring, 
mitigation and management system designed to reduce the impact of future earthquakes. 
 
The Project consists of the following parts, subject to such modifications thereof 
as the Borrower and the Bank may agree upon from time to time to achieve such 
objectives: 
 
Part A: Reconstruction of Housing, Social and Public 
Infrastructure 
 
1. 
Carrying out, through the provision of goods and the execution of works, of a 
program to assist households in the Project Area in repairing existing dwellings damaged 
by the Earthquakes and/or re-build new dwellings with seismic-resistant construction 
techniques. 
 
2. 
Carrying out, through the provision of goods and the execution of works, of a 
program to repair and/or reconstruct damaged schools and health clinics in the Project 
Area. 
 
3. 
Carrying out, through the provision of goods and the execution of works, of a 
program to repair and reconstruct damaged infrastructure systems and facilities in the 
Project Area including, without limitation, water and drainage networks, and telephone, 
power and road systems.
 
Part B:
Economic Recovery
Carrying out, through the provision of goods and the execution of works, of a 
program of investments in the agriculture and infrastructure sectors whose objective is to 
reduce the economic vulnerability to natural disasters of the population living in the 
Project Area. 
 
Part C:
Capacity Building and Project Management
1. 
Carrying out, through the provision of goods, consultants’ services and 
training, of a program to build capacity at the local and national levels, such program to 
consist, 
inter alia,
 of the carrying out of a review of national and local emergency 
experiences, and the conducting of a public awareness and media program on protective 
Page  14
-
13
-
measures to minimize risks in future disasters. 
2. 
Support, through the provision of goods, consultants’ services and training, to 
PMU and HF with a view to assisting in the management of their respective components 
of the Project and the carrying out of the activities financed thereunder. 
 
Part D
:
Disaster Insurance Preparation
Carrying out of feasibility studies and preparatory activities with a view to 
establishing a disaster insurance system, putting in place a suitable legal framework 
therefor and training potential service providers thereon.

Part B

International Competitive Bidding
Except as otherwise provided in Part C of this Section, goods and works shall be

Part C
:
Other Procurement Procedures
1. 
National Competitive Bidding
Goods estimated to cost less than $1,000,000 equivalent per contract, up to an 
aggregate amount not to exceed $15,000,000, and works estimated to cost less than 
$5,000,000 equivalent per contract, up to an aggregate amount not to exceed 
$140,000,000 equivalent, may be procured under contracts awarded in accordance with 
the provisions of paragraphs 3.3 and 3.4 of the Guidelines. 
 
2. 
International or National Shopping
Goods estimated to cost less than $100,000 equivalent per contract, up to an 
aggregate amount not to exceed $5,000,000 equivalent, may be procured under contracts 
awarded on the basis of international/national shopping procedures in accordance with 
the provisions of paragraphs 3.5 and 3.6 of the Guidelines. 
 
3. 
Procurement of Small Works
Small works, including incidental materials and tools required therefor, estimated 
to cost less than $100,000 equivalent per contract, up to an aggregate amount not to 
exceed $17,000,000 equivalent, shall be procured under lump-sum, fixed-price contracts 
awarded on the basis of quotations obtained from three (3) qualified domestic contractors 
in response to a written invitation. The invitation shall include a detailed description of 
the works, including basic specifications, the required completion date, a basic form of

Other Procedures for the Selection of Consultants
1. 
Selection Based on Consultants’ Qualifications
Services estimated to cost less than $100,000 equivalent per contract may be 
procured under contracts awarded in accordance with the provisions of paragraphs 3.1 
and 3.7 of the Consultant Guidelines. 
 
2. Individual 
Consultants
Services for technical assistance and training under Part B of the Project shall be 
procured under contracts awarded to individual consultants in accordance with the 
provisions of paragraphs 5.1 through 5.3 of the Consultant Guidelines. 
 
3. 
Single Source Selection
Services for short term technical assistance under the Project which are estimated 
to cost less than $50,000 equivalent per contract, may, with the Bank’s prior agreement, 
be procured in accordance with the provisions of paragraphs 3.8 through 3.11 of the 
Consultants Guidelines. 
 
Part D
:
Review by the Bank of the Selection of Consultants
1. Selection 
Planning
Prior to the issuance to consultants of any requests for proposals, the proposed 
plan for the selection of consultants under the Project shall be furnished to the Bank in 
accordance with the provisions of paragraph 1 of Appendix 1 to the Consultant 
Guidelines. Selection of all consultants’ services shall be undertaken in accordance with 
such selection plan as shall have been agreed with the Bank, and with the provisions of 
said paragraph 1.

(a)

SCHEDULE 5 
 
Implementation Program 
 
1. 
Implementation, Management and Coordination of the Project
(a) 
The Borrower shall vest in MOI the overall responsibility for the 
carrying out of the Project. MOI shall directly carry out Parts A.2, A.3, B and C of the 
Project and shall enter with HF into an implementation agreement, under terms and 
conditions approved by the Bank including the assumption by HF, against payment of a 
fee by MOI, of the responsibility, 
inter alia
,
for the carrying out of Part A.1 of the 
Project, and the procurement process under Parts A.1, A.2, A.3 and B of the Project. 
MOEAF shall be responsible for carrying out Part D of the Project. 
 
(b) 
Without prejudice to the provisions of paragraph 1 (a) above, the 
Implementation Agreement shall obligate HF: (i) to perform in accordance with the 
provisions of the Implementation Agreement all of the obligations of HF therein set forth; 
(ii) to take all action, including the provision of facilities, services and other resources, 
necessary or appropriate for the carrying out of Part A.1 of the Project; and (iii) not to 
take or permit to be taken any action which would prevent or interfere with the carrying 
out of Part A.1 of the Project. Furthermore, the Implementation Agreement shall obligate 
MOI to exercise its rights in such manner as to protect the interests of the Borrower and 
the Bank and to accomplish the purposes of the Loan, and, except as the Bank shall 
otherwise agree, the Implementation Agreement shall obligate MOI and HF not to assign, 
amend, abrogate or waive the Implementation Agreement or any provision thereof. 
 
(c) 
MOI and MOEAF shall be assisted in the carrying out of their 
responsibilities by a Project management unit whose main responsibilities shall include 
the day-to-day management of the Project, including the responsibility for the 
disbursement process thereunder, and the procurement process under Parts C and D of the 
Project. 
 
(d) 
The Borrower shall vest in NCNDR the responsibility for the overall 
management of the Project and the coordination of its implementation. NCNDR shall act 
as a steering committee for the Project and shall be chaired by a representative of MOI. 
Membership of NCNDR shall include representatives from MOEAF, MPO, the local Red 
Crescent Society, certain sector ministries, a number of research institutes and the 
provincial governments in the Project Area. 
 
(e) 
The Borrower shall, not later than September 30, 2003, cause MOI to 
employ two (2) individuals, whose qualifications and experience shall be satisfactory to 
the Bank, to assume the responsibilities of, respectively, financial director and financial 
management expert within PMU. 
 
(f) 
The Borrower shall, not later than September 30, 2003, open and 
maintain an account, with a commercial bank in the territory of the Borrower, where

2. 
Parts A and B of the Project
(a) 
In order to achieve the objectives of Parts A and B of the Project, the 
Borrower shall maintain arrangements satisfactory to the Bank pursuant to which HF and 
MOI shall select Sub-projects to be carried out under Part A.1 of the Project, by HF, and 
under Parts A.2, A.3 and B of the Project, by MOI. When presenting a Sub-project to the 
Bank for approval, HF or MOI, as the case may be, shall furnish to the Bank an 
application, in form and substance satisfactory to the Bank, together with an appraisal of 
the proposed Sub-project, including a description of the expenditures proposed to be 
financed out of the proceeds of the Loan. 
 
(b) 
Unless otherwise agreed by the Borrower and the Bank, no expenditures 
required for a Sub-project under Part A.1 of the Project shall be eligible for financing out 
of the proceeds of the Loan unless HF shall have first furnished to the Bank: (i) evidence, 
satisfactory to the Bank, that the damage or destruction sought to be addressed under the 
Sub-project shall have taken place after the occurrence of an Earthquake; (ii) the 
household whose dwelling is sought to be reconstructed under the Sub-project shall have 
secured an appropriate credit facility from a local commercial bank, in an amount 
acceptable to the Bank, to pay for part of the reconstruction costs; (iii) a design and cost 
estimates for the Sub-project, acceptable in substance to the Bank; (iv) evidence, 
satisfactory to the Bank, that the environmental screening for the Sub-project shall have 
been carried out in accordance with the provisions of the Framework; and (v) evidence, 
satisfactory to the Bank, of availability of local counterpart funds for the Sub-project in 
adequate amounts. 
 
(c) 
Unless otherwise agreed by the Borrower and the Bank, no expenditures 
required for a Sub-project under Parts A.2 and A.3 of the Project shall be eligible for 
financing out of the proceeds of the Loan unless MOI shall have first furnished to the 
Bank: (i) evidence, satisfactory to the Bank, that the damage or destruction sought to be 
addressed under the Sub-project shall have taken place after the occurrence of an 
Earthquake; (ii) a design and cost estimates for the Sub-project, acceptable in substance 
to the Bank; (iii) evidence, satisfactory to the Bank, that the environmental screening for 
the Sub-project shall have been carried out in accordance with the provisions of the 
Framework; (iv) evidence, satisfactory to the Bank, that environmental mitigation 
measures provided for under the Framework shall have been incorporated in the design of 
the Sub-project; and (v) evidence, satisfactory to the Bank, of availability of local 
counterpart funds for the Sub-project in adequate amounts. 
 
(d) 
Unless otherwise agreed by the Borrower and the Bank, no expenditures 
required for a Sub-project under Part B of the Project shall be eligible for financing out of 
the proceeds of the Loan unless MOI shall have first furnished to the Bank: (i) a study,

Special Account
 
1. 
For the purposes of this Schedule: 
 
(a) 
the term “eligible Categories” means Categories (1), (2) and (3) set forth 
in the table in paragraph 1 of Schedule 1 to this Agreement; 
 
(b) 
the term “eligible expenditures” means expenditures in respect of the 
reasonable cost of goods, works and services required for the Project and to be financed 
out of the proceeds of the Loan allocated from time to time to the eligible Categories in 
accordance with the provisions of Schedule 1 to this Agreement; and 
 
(c) 
the term “Authorized Allocation” means an amount equivalent to 
US$5,000,000 to be withdrawn from the Loan Account and deposited into the Special 
Account pursuant to paragraph 3 (a) of this Schedule, provided, however, that unless the 
Bank shall otherwise agree, the Authorized Allocation shall be limited to an amount 
equivalent to US$2,000,000 until the aggregate amount of withdrawals from the Loan 
Account plus the total amount of all outstanding special commitments entered into by the 
Bank pursuant to Section 5.02 of the General Conditions shall be equal to or exceed the 
equivalent of US$10,000,000. 
 
2. 
Payments out of the Special Account shall be made exclusively for eligible 
expenditures in accordance with the provisions of this Schedule. 
 
3. 
After the Bank has received evidence satisfactory to it that the Special Account 
has been duly opened, withdrawals of the Authorized Allocation and subsequent 
withdrawals to replenish the Special Account shall be made as follows:

to the Bank a request or requests for deposit into the Special Account of an amount or 
amounts which do not exceed the aggregate amount of the Authorized Allocation. On the 
basis of such request or requests, the Bank shall, on behalf of the Borrower, withdraw 
from the Loan Account and deposit into the Special Account such amount or amounts as 
the Borrower shall have requested.

(i) 
For replenishment of the Special Account, the Borrower shall 
furnish to the Bank requests for deposits into the Special 
Account at such intervals as the Bank shall specify. 
 
(ii) 
Prior to or at the time of each such request, the Borrower shall 
furnish to the Bank the documents and other evidence required 
pursuant to paragraph 4 of this Schedule for the payment or 
payments in respect of which replenishment is requested. On the

SCHEDULE 7 
 
Performance Indicators 
 
1. 
Number of damaged housing units that have been repaired or reconstructed in 
accordance with seismic standards acceptable to the Bank. 
 
2. 
Number of Sub-projects carried out under Part A.1 of the Project. 
 
3. 
Number of Sub-projects carried out under Parts A.2 and A.3 of the Project. 
 
4. 
Number of damaged and destroyed infrastructure facilities that have been 
repaired or reconstructed in accordance with seismic standards acceptable to the Bank. 
 
5. 
Number of Sub-projects carried out under Part B of the Project. 
 
6. 
Completion and submission to the Bank of a report, satisfactory to the Bank, 
detailing past national and local emergency response experiences. 
 
7. 
Launching of a public campaign on disaster preparedness, including the 
production of appropriate films and booklets, and the carrying out of educational sessions 
required therefor. 
 
8. 
Formulation of a comprehensive proposal, satisfactory in substance to the Bank, 
for the establishment of a local disaster insurance program. 
 
9. 
Carrying out a program to train local government staff on the enforcement of 
building codes. 
 
10. 
Preparation of draft legislation on natural disaster insurance and completion of a 
consultation process with relevant stakeholders.
