SCHEDULE 2
Description of the Project
The objectives of the Project are to assist the Borrower to: (a) reduce traffic 
bottlenecks on the national road network in selected urban areas in the Borrower's 
North Java corridor; (b) address environmental and social issues related to the usage 
of said network; (c) develop urban transport policies; (d) carry out the process of 
decentralization of responsibility for planning, design, construction, maintenance, 
management and regulation of urban transport from the Borrower's central level 
agencies to local governments; and (e) strengthen the technical and institutional 
capabilities of the Borrower's central level agencies and local governments.
The Project consists of the following parts, subject to such modifications 
thereof as the Borrower and the Bank may agree upon from time to time to achieve such 
objectives:
Part A:
Road Construction
1.
Carrying out of a program of road infrastructure works, including:
(a)
completion or new construction of ring roads and by\1epasses on new 
alignments;
(b)
widening and improvement of existing roads;
(c)
construction of culverts, bridges, road junctions, junction approach 
roads; and
Page  9
(d)
design and implementation of mitigation measures to address the 
environmental and the social impacts identified during the design, construction and 
supervision of works.
2.
Provision of traffic signal and related equipment for the respective DLLAJ of 
the Participating Local Governments.
3.
Resettlement and rehabilitation of Project Affected Persons under Part A. I 
above.
Part B:
Urban Management and Quality of Life Improvements
1.
Carrying out of a program of infrastructure works by Participating Local 
Governments as set out in traffic and environmental management plans for Project Towns 
covering existing roads and adjacent areas to be implemented upon completion of by\1e
passes.
2.
Carrying out of reviews of the impact of construction of by\1epasses on land use 
and transport investment plans.
3.
Development of measures required for the protection of the right\1eof\1eway of the 
by\1epasses constructed under Part A. 1 above.
4.
Strengthening of the operational capabilities of selected Participating Local 
Governments in routine road maintenance, including provision of equipment and 
training.
5.
Resettlement and rehabilitation of Project Affected Persons under Part B. I 
above.
Part C:
Institutional and Human Resource Development
1.
Strengthening of the operational and implementation capabilities of the DLLAJ of 
each Participating Local Government, including the provision of advisory services.
2.
Improvement of the skills of staff of Participating Local Governments assigned 
to their respective DLLAJ through the provision of technical training.
3.
Strengthening of the environmental impact assessment capabilities of BINKOT, 
including the provision of advisory services.
Part D:
Urban Road Program Development
Establishment and updating of a management system, at the national level, for an 
urban road investment program, including the provision of advisory services.
Part E:
Policy Formulation and Articulation
1.
Preparation of an urban transport policy statement, including the provision of 
advisory services.
2. 
Preparation of draft policies, procedures and guidelines on the planning, design 
and management of urban roads applicable at the national and local levels, including 
the provision of advisory services.
