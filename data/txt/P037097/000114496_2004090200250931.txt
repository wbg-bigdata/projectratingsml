SCHEDULE 2
Description of the Project
The objective of the Project is to assist the Borrower in improving the quality 
and coverage of junior secondary education in its provinces of East Java and East Nusa 
Tenggara, through the expansion of access to junior secondary education in a 
cost-effective and equitable way, the improvement of teaching at junior secondary 
schools, and the strengthening of the management capacity at all levels of the 
education system.
The Project consists of the following parts, subject to such modifications 
thereof as the Borrower and the Bank may agree upon from time to time to achieve such 
objectives:
PART A:
Increasing Access to Junior Secondary Education
 
1.
Increasing the access of students to junior secondary education through:
 
(a)
construction in remote areas of East Java of six-classroom and 
three-classroom schools, and teacher dormitories at new three-classroom schools and at 
existing schools, and provision of related furniture; and
(b)
construction in remote areas of East Nusa Tenggara of six-classroom and 
three-classroom schools, additional classrooms in existing schools, and teacher 
dormitories at new three-classroom schools, and provision of related furniture.
2.
Provision and distribution of learning and reference books and related furniture 
to SMP Terbuka learning centers, and training of supervising tutors.
3.
Carrying out of a program to provide scholarships based on family income and 
student school performance to junior secondary students in East Java and East Nusa 
Tenggara.
Page  8
4.
Design and implementation of a program to develop parents' awareness of the 
benefits of secondary education, and teachers and leaders' commitment to the 
objectives of the Project, through analysis, design and dissemination of printed and 
audio-visual messages.
PART B:
Improving the Quality of Education
 
Improving the quality of education in East Java and East Nusa Tenggara, through:
1.
Provision of PKG and MGMP training to teachers in East Java and East Nusa 
Tenggara, including training in the appropriate teaching methods, student learning 
issues, and special problems affecting teaching in remote areas.
2.
Carrying out a program of employment of contract teachers in core academic areas 
for rural schools where retaining teachers is difficult. 
3.
Design and implementation of a student assessment system in East Java and East 
Nusa Tenggara, including provision of related training and technical assistance, and 
provision of related equipment.
4.
Construction of library rooms in junior secondary schools in East Java and East 
Nusa Tenggara, and provision of reference books, reading books, supplementary books 
for textbook expansion, and furniture to such libraries.
5.
Provision of science equipment and teaching aids for mathematics and social 
sciences for new and existing schools.
6.
Carrying out of a study in East Java and East Nusa Tenggara to identify the best 
education strategy for the teaching of science and technology.
PART C:
Strengthening Education Management
 
1.
Training of administrative staff at the central, provincial, district, 
sub-district and school level for purposes of strengthening their implementation and 
general managerial capacity; publication of relevant handbooks; and provision of 
technical assistance.
2.
Strengthening the capacity of the East Java and East Nusa Tenggara provincial, 
district and sub-district education administration to manage and use information, 
including (i) forecasting, planning and monitoring by Kanwils  and  Kandeps/Kanins; 
(ii) data processing by Dikmenum and
Kanwils; and (iii) school education data management by Kandeps/Kanins, through 
provision of technical assistance, training for relevant administrative staff, and 
provision of related equipment.
PART D:
Project Management
 
Strengthening the capacity of the Provincial Project Implementation Units in 
East Java and East Nusa Tenggara through the provision of technical assistance, staff 
training and office equipment.
*   *   *
The Project is expected to be completed by December 31, 2001.

To the extent practicable, contracts for goods shall be grouped in bid packages

Other Procurement Procedures
 
1.
National Competitive Bidding
 
(a)
Goods (excluding furniture) estimated to cost less than $200,000 
equivalent per contract, up to an aggregate amount not to exceed $4,000,000 
equivalent, may be procured under contracts awarded in accordance with the provisions 
of paragraphs 3.3 and 3.4 of the Guidelines.

Except as provided in paragraphs 2 and 3 below, furniture and works shall 
be procured under contracts awarded in accordance with the provisions of paragraphs

National Shopping
 
Equipment and educational materials estimated to cost less than $50,000 
equivalent per contract, up to an aggregate amount not to exceed $2,300,000

books, up to an aggregate amount not to exceed $10,000,000, may be procured under 
contracts awarded on the basis of national shopping procedures in accordance with the 
provisions of paragraphs 3.5 and 3.6 of the Guidelines.
3.
Procurement of Small Works
 
Works estimated to cost less than $50,000 equivalent per contract, up to an 
amount not to exceed $25,500,000 equivalent, may be procured under lump-sum, 
fixed-price contracts awarded on the basis of quotations obtained from three (3) 
Page  11
qualified domestic contractors in response to a written invitation. The invitation 
shall include a detailed description of the works, including basic specifications, the 
required completion date, a basic form of agreement acceptable to the Bank, and

offers the lowest price quotation for the required work, and who has the experience 
and resources to complete the contract successfully.
