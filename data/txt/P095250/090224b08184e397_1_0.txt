                         Project Development Objectives
                         Health Services & Social Assistance (P095250)
                         Project Development Objective (from Project Appraisal Document)
                         The proposed Project will be an integral part of a larger and longer-term program of the Government to improve the effectiveness of health and social assistance
                         systems in Moldova. In addition to the proposed IDA credit, several donors including IDA, EU, SIDA, DfID, CEB and relevant UN agencies, will through coordinated but
                         parallel financed operations, support the Government initiatives in this area. The overall project objective is to promote the Government's program to increase access to
                         quality and efficient health services with the aim of decreasing premature mortality and disability for the local population and improve the targeting of social transfers
                         and services to the poor in line with the MTEF for 2007-2009.

                         The original credit (Cr. 4320-MD) in the amount of SDR 11.3 million (USD 17 million equivalent) effective on September 5, 2007 has demonstrated good implementation progress.

                         An additional credit in an amount of SDR 6.6 million (USD 10.2 million equivalent) to the Moldova Health Services and Social Assistance Project (HSSAP, P095250) was approved by
                         the Board of Executive Directors on December 8, 2011, legal agreement signed on December 20, 2011 and became effective on 25 May 2012 upon its ratification by the Moldovan
Public Disclosure Copy




                         Parliament.
                         In addition, the closing date for the HSSA project has been extended to August 31, 2013, and the project has been restructured as follows:
                         (a) results framework has been revised, and key performance indicators adjusted towards outcomes that are measurable and more closely aligned with project activities;
                         (b) project description modified to include the new activities financed under the AF.

                         The additional funds would help finance the costs for the rehabilitation of primary Rural Health Centers (RHCs) and will expand activities that scale-up impact and the development
                         effectiveness of this complex, but well-performing project.

                         The project activities have been aligned with the MoH Policy Roadmap “accelerating reforms: addressing the needs of the health area through investment policies? [2012-2014]
                         approved by the ministerial order in February 2012. Furthermore, some reallocations across activities have been authorized to further support the implementation of the new payment
                         system for hospitals based on DRGs, and to ensure raising awareness on health sector reforms amongst general public.

                         Over the last few months, implementation of project activities has continued to proceed satisfactorily, and disbursement rates have reached over 90 percent of the original loan
                         amount and 67% of the Additional Financing:

                         a) procurement packages for a new batch of primary care centers to be renovated using the additional funds have been prepared and selection of construction companies is
                         underway. To date, 29 RHCs out of total 35 RHCs have been contracted;

                         b) selection of consultants for few key critical technical assistance tasks financed from the additional financing has been completed, including: i) feasibility study for the
                         decentralization of chemotherapy and radiotherapy services for cancer patients to Cahul and Balti regional hospitals; ii) training of PHC doctors in management of autonomous health
                         facilities; iii) further development of clinical protocols for primary care, and utilization of protocols already developed; iv) plan for regionalization of hospital services, and further
                         rationalization of the hospital network in Chisinau; v) plan for development of long term and geriatric services in Moldova.

                         c) works for the construction of the new surgical ward within the National Republican Hospitals that started in March 2012 is under way;

                         d) preparation for the nationwide launch of the DRG-based payment system for hospital care has been taken over by National Health Insurance Company. In addition, the Ministry of
                         Health reallocated project funds in order to continue support to implementation of DRG system in Moldova.
