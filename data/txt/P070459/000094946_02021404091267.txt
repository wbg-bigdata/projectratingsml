2.3.6 Land Desertification Status
The main feature of the land desertification on both sides of the routes is soil desertification by wind
erosion.
Of the types of land desertification along the improvement highways, the principal types are of the
medium and high extent, the features of which: the soil of the ground surface desertification by wind
erosion, with the granule blown, raised and carried far away from the original ground surface,
resulting in soil granulated, ground surface component matter roughening, the physical sand granule
content of soil higher than 85%.

than 70 days, and the peak values of the yearly wind speed, of the strong wind days and of the sand
storm days, all appear from March to May; meanwhile extremely rare rainfall causing droughts to
come simultaneously with such strong winds, added with rather rough soil quality rendering poor
anti-erosion capacity, and the over-reclamation of land by humans, all these lead to extremely
serious soil desertification by wind erosion in the both sides of the rotite. The area of land
desertification by wind erosion takes up more than 80% of the assessed area.

Description of Culture Relics of Local Highway Network
Jan 11,2002
According to Culture Relics Protection Law of People's
Republic of China, Institute of Cultural Relics and Archaeology of
Inner Mongolia organized a census of the five sections of local
highway networks. First, they looked through the information of the
census conducted in 1982 in Inner Mongolia Autonomous Region,
which shows there are about 500 places of culture relics distributed
around Wulanchabu League and about 400 places around Baotou. The
five sections of local highway network improvement program have no
relations to the above-mentioned places. As to the information of the
census in 1982, the information could not brought into open because
the concerns of robbery and excavation of the ancient tombs. The
information is kept in the data bank of Culture Relics Institute.
