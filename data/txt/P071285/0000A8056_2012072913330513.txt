                         Project Development Objectives
                         Project Development Objective (from Project Appraisal Document)

                         The primary objectives of the project are to: (i) improve rural water supply and sanitation sector institutional performance and mainstream the "Fund Board" (Rural
                         Water Supply and Sanitation Fund Development Board) approach in the Government's system, and (ii) support communities to form inclusive local water supply and
                         sanitation user groups that can plan, implement, and operate drinking water and sanitation infrastructure that delivers sustainable health, hygiene and productivity
                         benefits to rural households.

                         Implementation Status Overview
                         A short Implementation Support Review was conducted by the Bank team between July 4-6, 2012 to clarify a small number of “end of project? issues. The implementation of the
                         Project has progressed well. Till date, 1422 schemes covering a population of about 1.18 million has been completed against the project target of 1463 schemes covering a
                         population of 1.04 million. 86% of HHs in the Project area have latrines as against the National rural average of 38%. Women's Technical Support Services groups have been formed
