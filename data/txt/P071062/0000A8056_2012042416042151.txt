                         Project Development Objectives
                         Project Development Objective (from Project Appraisal Document)

                         The revised Project Development Objective is to improve the quality of health services in a sustainable manner in order to contribute to the progress towards achieving
                         the health-related Millennium Development Goals (MDGs) of reducing child and maternal mortality and combating communicable diseases.

                         Implementation Status Overview
                         The Project is moving forward towards achieving its Development Objectives. Maternal mortality ratio has decreased gradually from 550 deaths per 100,000 live births in 2002, to 385


                                                                                                                                                                                                          Page 1 of 6

                         in 2006, to 196 in 2008, to 118 in 2009, and to 94 deaths per 100,000 live births in 2010. The child mortality rate has been reduced from 103 deaths per 1000 live births in 2002 to 67
                         deaths per 1000 live births in 2006. Medically assisted delivery reached 58 percent in 2010 compared to 44 percent in 2002. Moreover, the proportion of children 12-23 months of age
                         who are vaccinated with DPT3 before 12 months of age has increased from 45 percent in 2002 to 90 percent in 2010. In terms of combating communicable diseases, HIV/AIDS
                         prevalence among young pregnant women (15 to 24 years old) has decreased to 1.4 percent in 2010, compared to 2.1 percent in 2007 and 2.9 percent in 2002. In addition, the total
Public Disclosure Copy




                         number of students enrolled in the High Institute of Health Sciences (HIHS) has increased to 1098 students in 2011 compared to 515 students in 2008. The total number of
                         insecticide treated bed nets distributed to households in high transmission areas has increased to 582,500 bed nets in 2011 compared to 138,000 bed nets in 2008.

                         The preparation of the Health Survey, conducted by the Pan Arab Project for Family Health (PAPFAM) under project financing, is ongoing. The field work started at end of March
                         2012 and its preliminary results are expected by mid June 2012. This Health Survey will provide updated figures for the following PDO indicators: (i) maternal mortality ratio, (ii) child
                         mortality rate, and (iii) HIV/AIDS prevalence rate.
