      AGREEMENT, dated June 5, 1991, between MAURITIUS (the
Borrower) and INTERNATIONAL BANK FOR RECONSTRUCTION AND DEVELOPMENT
(the Bank).
      WHEREAS (A) the Borrower, having satisfied itself as to the
feasibility and priority of the Project described in Schedule 2 to
this Agreement, has requested the Bank to assist in the financing of
the Project;
      WHEREAS the Bank has agreed, on the basis, inter alia, of the
foregoing, to extend the Loan to the Borrower upon the terms and
conditions set forth in this Agreement;
      NOW THEREFORE the parties hereto hereby agree as follows:
                             ARTICLE I
                  General Conditions; Definitions
      Section 1.01. The "General Conditions Applicable to Loan and
Page  2
Guarantee Agreements" of the Bank, dated January 1, 1985, with the
modifications set forth below (the General Conditions) constitute an
integral part of this Agreement:
      (a)   the last sentence of Section 3.02 is deleted; and
      (b)   sub-paragraph (k) of Section 6.02 is relettered as sub-
paragraph (l) and a new sub-paragraph (k) is added to read:
            "An extraordinary situation shall have arisen under
      which any further withdrawals under the Loan would be
      inconsistent with the provisions of Article III, Section 3 of
      the Bank's Articles of Agreement."
      Section 1.02. Unless the context otherwise requires, the
several terms defined in the General Conditions have the respective
meanings therein set forth and the following additional terms have
the following meanings:
      (a)   "IA" means the Borrower's Irrigation Authority; and
      (b)   "MAFNR" means the Borrower's Ministry of Agriculture,
Fisheries and Natural Resources.
                            ARTICLE II
                             The Loan
      Section 2.01. The Bank agrees to lend to the Borrower, on the
terms and conditions set forth or referred to in the Loan Agreement,
various currencies that shall have an aggregate value equivalent to
the amount of ten million dollars ($10,000,000), being the sum of
withdrawals of the proceeds of the Loan, with each withdrawal valued
by the Bank as of the date of such withdrawal.
      Section 2.02. The amount of the Loan may be withdrawn from the
Loan Account in accordance with the provisions of Schedule 1 to this
Agreement for expenditures made (or, if the Bank shall so agree, to
be made) in respect of the reasonable cost of goods and services
required for the Project described in Schedule 2 to this Agreement
and to be financed out of the proceeds of the Loan.
      Section 2.03. The Closing Date shall be June 30, 1999 or such
later date as the Bank shall establish. The Bank shall promptly
notify the Borrower of such later date.
      Section 2.04. The Borrower shall pay to the Bank a commitment
charge at the rate of three-fourths of one percent (3/4 of 1%) per
annum on the principal amount of the Loan not withdrawn from time to
time.
      Section 2.05. (a) The Borrower shall pay interest on the
principal amount of the Loan withdrawn and outstanding from time to
time, at a rate for each Interest Period equal to the Cost of
Qualified Borrowings determined in respect of the preceding
Semester, plus one-half of one percent (1/2 of 1%). On each of the
dates specified in Section 2.06 of this Agreement, the Borrower
shall pay interest accrued on the principal amount outstanding
during the preceding Interest Period, calculated at the rate
applicable during such Interest Period.
      (b)   As soon as practicable after the end of each Semester,
the Bank shall notify the Borrower of the Cost of Qualified
Borrowings determined in respect of such Semester.
      (c)   For the purposes of this Section:
            (i)   "Interest Period" means a six-month period ending
                  on the date immediately preceding each date
                  specified in Section 2.06 of this Agreement,
                  beginning with the Interest Period in which this
                  Agreement is signed.
Page  3
            (ii)  "Cost of Qualified Borrowings" means the cost, as
                  reasonably determined by the Bank and expressed
                  as a percentage per annum, of the outstanding
                  borrowings of the Bank drawn down after June 30,
                  1982, excluding such borrowings or portions
                  thereof as the Bank has allocated to fund: (A)
                  the Bank's investments; and (B) loans which may
                  be made by the Bank after July 1, 1989 bearing
                  interest rates determined otherwise than as
                  provided in paragraph (a) of this Section.
            (iii) "Semester" means the first six months or the
                  second six months of a calendar year.
      (d)   On such date as the Bank may specify by no less than six
months' notice to the Borrower, paragraphs (a), (b) and (c) (iii) of
this Section shall be amended to read as follows:
            "(a)  The Borrower shall pay interest on the principal
      amount of the Loan withdrawn and outstanding from time to
      time, at a rate for each Quarter equal to the Cost of
      Qualified Borrowings determined in respect of the preceding
      Quarter, plus one-half of one percent (1/2 of 1%). On each of
      the dates specified in Section 2.06 of this Agreement, the
      Borrower shall pay interest accrued on the principal amount
      outstanding during the preceding Interest Period, calculated
      at the rates applicable during such Interest Period."
            "(b)  As soon as practicable after the end of each
      Quarter, the Bank shall notify the Borrower of the Cost of
      Qualified Borrowings determined in respect of such Quarter."
            "(c)  (iii) `Quarter' means a three-month period
      commencing on January 1, April 1, July 1 or October 1 in a
      calendar year."
      Section 2.06. Interest and other charges shall be payable
semiannually on February 15 and August 15 in each year.
      Section 2.07. The Borrower shall repay the principal amount of
the Loan in accordance with the amortization schedule set forth in
Schedule 3 to this Agreement.
                            ARTICLE III
                     Execution of the Project
      Section 3.01. (a) The Borrower declares its commitment to the
objectives of the Project as set forth in Schedule 2 to this
Agreement and, to this end, shall carry out the Project with due
diligence and efficiency and in conformity with appropriate
administrative, financial and agricultural practices, and shall
provide, promptly as needed, the funds, facilities, services and
other resources required for the Project.
      (b)   Without limitation upon the provisions of paragraph (a)
of this Section and, except as the Borrower and the Bank shall
otherwise agree, the Borrower shall carry out the Project in
accordance with the Implementation Program set forth in Schedule 5
to this Agreement.
      Section 3.02. Except as the Bank shall otherwise agree,
procurement of the goods, works and consultants' services required
for the Project and to be financed out of the proceeds of the Loan
shall be governed by the provisions of Schedule 4 to this Agreement.
      Section 3.03. The Borrower shall undertake, three years after
the Effective Date, an evaluation of the implementation of the
Project on the basis of terms of reference satisfactory to the Bank.
The Borrower shall, within three months of the completion of the
evaluation, submit an evaluation report to the Bank for its review
Page  4
and comments. The evaluation report shall include the Borrower's
plans to rectify identified constraints.

                            SCHEDULE 2
                    Description of the Project
      The objectives of the Project are to improve the Borrower's
capabilities for sectoral management, policy formulation and for the
delivery of support services in its agricultural sector.
      The Project consists of the following parts, subject to such
modifications thereof as the Borrower and the Bank may agree upon
from time to time to achieve such objectives:
Part A:     Management
      Strengthening of agricultural sector management capacity
through:
1.    the establishment of a Consultative Committee on Agriculture
supported by a permanent secretariat;
2.    establishment of an Agricultural Policy Analysis Unit within
MAFNR; and
3.    acquisition and installation of computerized agricultural and
management information systems.
Part B:     Agricultural Support Services
      Strengthening of agricultural support services through:
1.    the restructuring of the research and extension services
within MAFNR, including acquisition of, inter alia, equipment,
computers and library facilities and construction of an office
building; and
2.    acquisition of computing facilities for the coordination of
the Borrower's agricultural research activities.
Part C:     Irrigation Services
      Strengthening the institutional framework and operations of IA
through:
1.    restructuring of its organization, including training programs
for its staff and for beneficiary farmers;
2.    rehabilitation of the Western Plains Project main canal; and
3.    carrying out three operations, including a pilot operation, to
test the technical and economic feasibility of a low cost approach
to irrigation.
Part D:     Miscellaneous Support Services and Studies
1.    Development of a marketing strategy and investment program for
small producers on the basis of recommendations of the small
producer efficiency study.
2.    Development of the export capacity of the Agricultural
Page  9
Marketing Board and improvement of its marketing operations.
3.    Development of an integrated framework for providing services,
including training, financial, marketing and technical support and
services to small-scale producers in the agri-business sector.
4.    Development of the management capacity of farmer groups, the
land area management units and the water user groups in irrigation
areas.

                            SCHEDULE 4
               Procurement and Consultants' Services
Section I.  Procurement of Goods and Works
Part A:     International Competitive Bidding
1.    Except as provided in Part C hereof, goods and works shall be
procured under contracts awarded in accordance with procedures
consistent with those set forth in Sections I and II of the
"Guidelines for Procurement under IBRD Loans and IDA Credits"
published by the Bank in May 1985 (the Guidelines).
2.    To the extent practicable, contracts for goods shall be
grouped in bid packages estimated to cost the equivalent of $100,000
or more each.
Part B:     Preference for Domestic Manufacturers
      In the procurement of goods in accordance with the procedures
described in Part A.1 hereof, goods manufactured in Mauritius may be
granted a margin of preference in accordance with, and subject to,
the provisions of paragraphs 2.55 and 2.56 of the Guidelines and
paragraphs 1 through 4 of Appendix 2 thereto.
Part C:     Other Procurement Procedures
1.    Groups of items estimated to cost less than the equivalent of
$100,000 per contract up to an aggregate amount not to exceed the
equivalent of $200,000 may be procured under contracts awarded on
the basis of competitive bidding, advertised locally, in accordance
with procedures satisfactory to the Bank.
2.    Small items of goods estimated to cost less than $25,000 each
up to an aggregate amount of $200,000 equivalent may be procured
under contracts awarded on the basis of comparison of price
quotations solicited from a list of at least three suppliers
eligible under the Guidelines, in accordance with procedures
acceptable to the Bank.
Part D:    Review by the Bank of Procurement Decisions
1.    Review of invitations to bid and of proposed awards and final
contracts:
      (a)   With respect to each contract estimated to cost the
equivalent of $100,000 or more, the procedures set forth in
paragraphs 2 and 4 of Appendix 1 to the Guidelines shall apply.
      (b)   With respect to each contract not governed by the
preceding paragraph, the procedures set forth in paragraphs 3 and 4
of Appendix 1 to the Guidelines shall apply.
      (c)   The provisions of the preceding subparagraph (b) shall
not apply to contracts on account of which the Bank has authorized
withdrawals on the basis of statements of expenditure.
2.    The figure of 15% is hereby specified for purposes of
paragraph 4 of Appendix 1 to the Guidelines.
Section II.  Employment of Consultants
Page  11
      In order to assist the Borrower in carrying out the Project,
the Borrower shall employ consultants whose qualifications,
experience and terms and conditions of employment shall be
satisfactory to the Bank. Such consultants shall be selected in
accordance with principles and procedures satisfactory to the Bank
on the basis of the "Guidelines for the Use of Consultants by World
Bank Borrowers and by the World Bank as Executing Agency" published
by the Bank in August 1981.
                            SCHEDULE 5
                      Implementation Program
1.    MAFNR shall, subject to the provisions of this Agreement, be
responsible for the overall coordination, monitoring and supervision
of activities to be carried out under the Project. MAFNR shall be
assisted in this connection by the Administrative Secretariat and
the Agricultural Policy Analysis Unit referred to under Part A.2 of
the Project.
Part A:
2.    The Consultative Committee on Agriculture referred to in Part
A.1 of the Project shall be chaired by the Borrower's Minister of
Agriculture and shall include in its membership, the Permanent
Secretary of MAFNR, representatives of other Ministries of the
Borrower, representatives of farmers from the sugar and non-sugar
sectors and the executive secretary of the secretariat who shall be
its Secretary. The Committee shall meet at least once a year to
consider and assess the performance of agriculture-related services,
review sectoral performance and developments and make
recommendations on policies, priorities and allocation of resources.
3.    The Agriculture Policy Analysis Unit referred to in Part A.2
of the Project shall primarily support the management and
coordination arrangements in the agricultural sector through
analytical planning, evaluation and information services. The Unit
shall take all necessary action to work closely with the Borrower's
organizations under MAFNR, its Ministry of Economic Planning and
Development and other relevant institutions.
4.    The Unit shall carry out the day-to-day monitoring and
coordination of the Project and shall be responsible for preparing
half yearly reports on progress made in the carrying out of the
Project based on submissions from the Project implementation
agencies.
Part B:
5.    The Directorate of Agricultural Research and Extension and the
farmers' service centers shall coordinate their respective
activities. In this connection, they shall, inter alia, meet at
least quarterly to exchange information on their respective work and
training programs. In addition, staff from each of these entities
may attend workshops arranged by each entity.
6.    The Directorate and the Agricultural Services (AS) shall also
coordinate their activities. For this purpose a Management Committee
shall be established comprising the Chief Agricultural Officer and
the Principal Agricultural Officer of AS and the Director, the
Assistant Directors and the Extension and Training Coordinator of
the Directorate. The Committee shall meet as frequently as necessary
to carry out its terms of reference and shall establish such
technical committees as are required to deal, inter alia, with
quality control in input supply services, regulatory and production
activities, flow of research findings and feedback from extension
services.
7.    MAFNR shall cause a meeting to be held of Agricultural
Scientists each year under the chairmanship of the Minister of
Agriculture or his designate. Such meetings shall be coordinated by
the Borrower's Food and Agricultural Research Council and a copy of
Page  12
the proceedings of each such annual meeting shall be furnished to
the Bank for its review and comments.
Part C:
8.    The Borrower shall establish by not later than December 31,
1991, a Water Resources Committee which shall be comprised of senior
officials of MAFNR, its Ministries of Energy, Finance, Environment
and Land Use, Economic Planning and Development, and the Central
Water Authority and IA. The Committee shall, inter alia:
      (a)   establish an Action Program for finalizing the Master
Plan for the Use of Water Resources to cover a 40-50 year period;
      (b)   formulate the long-term strategy for potable water
supply and irrigation for Mauritius;
      (c)   formulate equitable allocation of water resources
between different users;
      (d)   make recommendations on water charges; and
      (e)   follow-up implementation of the Master Plan for the Use
of Water Resources and water resources allocation.
9.    The physical implementation of Part C of the Project shall be
carried out by IA.
10.   (a)   The Borrower shall cause IA to prepare and furnish to
the Bank for approval by:
            (i)   December 31, 1991, specification and bid
                  documents for works, vehicles and equipment
                  related to Part C of the Project; and
           (ii)   December 31, 1991, detailed design specifications
                  and bid documents for rehabilitation works
                  related to Part C of the Project.
      (b)   The Borrower shall cause IA to prepare and furnish to
the Bank by November 1, 1994, for its review and comments after the
implementation of the pilot program of Water Users Associations,
IA's assessment of the program and its proposals for its further
expansion.
      (c)   The Borrower shall cause IA to prepare and furnish to
the Bank for its review and comment by June 30, 1994, a study to
assess the system of charges for water use for irrigation, including
recommendations, if any on the improvement of the system.
11.   The Borrower shall cause IA to prepare and furnish to the Bank
for its review and comments, by not later than May 31, 1992, a
detailed training program.
12.   The Bank and the Borrower shall, by November 1, 1994 review
the implementation of the pilot operation referred to in Part C.3 of
the Project in terms of the potential for further expansion.
