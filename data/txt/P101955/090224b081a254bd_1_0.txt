                         Implementation Status Overview
                         1. The project has succeeded in rehabilitating 28 water sub-projects supporting livestock-dependent pastorals communities and is currently building three additional, thanks to the
                         additional financing, rehabilitated/upgraded six livestock markets and supported 31 Village Development Committees (VDCs), including training and capacity building packages for
                         pastoral communities and private veterinarian to enable them access financial resources. To dates the project benefited more than 160,000 people ( were 35% of them are women)
Public Disclosure Copy




                         in the six targeted localities of the following four key pastoral states: (i) North Kordofan State (Gebaish, En Nuhood and Abu Zabad localities); (ii) Blue Nile (Damazin locality); (iii)
                         Sinnar (Singa/Abu Hujar locality); and (iv) White Nile State (Al-Gabalain locality). The project's closing date was successfully extended up to June 30, 2013 and received US$1.8
                         million additional financing to complete the target number of water points, along the livestock routes, and complete the infrastructure designed for livestock markets.

                         2. The PDO is to “improve livestock production and marketing in selected rain fed areas of Central and Eastern Sudan?. The project is structured around pilot activities that address
                         pastoral communities’ priority needs in support of livestock production and marketing.. This objective is in line with the CAADP pillars to provide access to reliable water source,
                         increase market access and trade-related activities, strengthen and sustain pastoral livelihood, introduce appropriate technologies in support to livestock productivity and diversify
                         sources of income for the targeted pastoral communities.
