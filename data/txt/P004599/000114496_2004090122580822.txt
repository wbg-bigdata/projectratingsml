SCHEDULE 2
Description of the Project
The objective of the Project is to assist the Borrower in establishing and 
thereafter operating a modern computerized integrated tax administration system 
throughout its territory.
The Project consists of the following parts, subject to such modifications 
thereof as the Borrower and the Bank may agree upon
from time to time to achieve such objectives:
Part A:
BIR Computerized Tax Administration System
1.
Establishing a computerized integrated tax administration system to process and 
audit all tax returns, including personal individual income tax, corporate income tax, 
excise tax, other percentage taxes, withholding tax and value added tax.
Page  8
2.
Provision of computer hardware and software, and related data-entry processing 
equipment and materials necessary for establishing the tax administration system and 
collecting the taxes referred to in Part A.1 above, comprising, inter alia: (a) a 
mainframe computer at BIR's national office; (b) communication and microcomputer 
systems and data©entry and computing facilities at about nineteen (19) BIR regional 
offices and at about forty-four (44) BIR revenue district offices; and (c) compatible 
computer software in respect of the foregoing.
3.
Provision of priority office equipment and related materials and utility 
vehicles necessary for the operation and management of BIR.
Part B:
BOC Computerized Tax Administration System
1.
Establishing a computerized integrated tax administration system to process 
customs taxes and other customs related transactions and improve the overall 
processing efficiency in respect of the import and export of goods within the 
Borrower's territory.
2.
Provision of computer hardware and system software, and related data-entry 
processing equipment and material, for establishing the tax administration system and 
collecting the taxes referred to in Part B.1 above, comprising, inter alia: (a) two 
(2) separate computers for software development and integrating all tax data at the 
Office of the Commissioner of BOC, and providing related data support and valuation 
systems; (b) microcomputer systems for the Borrower's: Manila Port, Food Terminal Inc. 
of the Manila Port, Manila International Container Port, Batangas Port, Cebu Port, 
Manila International Airport and Cebu International Airport; (c) microcomputer systems 
for integrated processing of export-related taxes; and (d) compatible computer 
software in respect of the foregoing.
3.
Provision of a customs application software system for, customs tax 
administration, including provision of related technical training for operating, 
maintaining and customization and developing system.
4.
Provision of priority office and laboratory equipment and utility vehicles 
necessary for the operation and management of BOC.
Part C:
Institutional Strenghtening
1.
Carrying out of a study, based on terms of reference satisfactory to the Bank, 
on the organizational improvements of BIR's and BOC's administration and operations.
2.
Provision of technical assistance services to each of BIR and BOC in: (a) 
supervising the implementation of their respective parts of the Project; and (b) 
strengthening their respective institutional capabilities, organization and procedures 
for operating and maintaining their corresponding tax administration system referred 
to in Parts A and B above, respectively.
*   *   *   *
The Project is expected to be completed by December 31, 1998.

Section I.
Procurement of Goods
Part A:
International Competitive Bidding
1.
Except as provided in Part C hereof, the computer hardware and software under

responsibility contracts awarded in accordance with procedures consistent with those 
set forth in Sections I and II of the "Guidelines for Procurement under IBRD Loans and

responsibility contract shall be at a fixed foreign currency or Peso price and shall, 
inter alia, require the corresponding contractor to carry out the initial operation, 
training and maintenance for a period of up to five (5) years. For fixed-price 
contracts, the invitation to bid referred to in paragraph 2.13 of the Guide¬lines 
shall provide that, when contract award is delayed beyond the original bid validity 
period, the successful bidder's bid price will be increased for each week of delay by 
two (2) pre©disclosed correction factors acceptable to the Bank, one (1) to be applied 
to all foreign currency components, and the other to the local currency component of 
the bid price. Such an increase shall not be taken into account in the bid evaluation.
2.
Contracts for goods under Part A.3 and B.3 of the Project shall be awarded in 
accordance with procedures consistent with those set forth in Sections I and II of the 
Guidelines.
Part B:
Preference for Domestic Manufacturers
In the procurement of goods in accordance with the procedures described in Part 
A.2 hereof, goods manufactured in the Republic of the Philippines may be granted a 
margin of preference in accordance with, and subject to, the provisions of paragraphs 
2.55 and 2.56 of the Guidelines and paragraphs 1 through 4 of Appendix 2 thereto.

Other Procurement Procedures
1.
Limited International Bidding

international bidding procedures on the basis of evaluation and comparison of bids 
obtained from at least three (3) qualified suppliers eligible under the Guidelines, 
and in accordance with procedures set forth in Sections I and II of the Guidelines 
(excluding paragraphs 2.8, 2.9, 2.55, 2.56 thereof).
2.
Direct Contracting

Part B (3) of the Project, may be procured after direct negotiations with the supplier

(a)

and (6) set forth in the table in paragraph 1 of Schedule 1 to this Agreement;
    (b)
The term "Eligible Expenditures" means expenditures in respect of the 
reasonable cost of goods and services required for the Project and to be financed out 
of the proceeds of the Loan allocated from time to time to the Eligible Categories in 
accordance with the provisions of Schedule 1 to this Agreement; and
    (c)
The term "Authorized Allocation" means an amount equivalent
Page  13
to: (i) one million dollars ($1,000,000) for the BIR Special Account; and (ii) one 
million dollars ($1,000,000) for the BOC Special Account, all to be withdrawn from the 
Loan Account and deposited in the respective Special Account pursuant to paragraph 3 
(a) of this Schedule.
2.
Payments out of the Special Accounts shall be made exclusively for Eligible 
Expenditures in accordance with the provisions of this Schedule.
3.
After the Bank has received evidence satisfactory to it that the Special 
Accounts have been duly opened, withdrawals of the Authorized Allocation and 
subsequent withdrawals to replenish the Special Account shall be made as follows:
    (a)
For withdrawals of the Authorized Allocation, the Borrower shall furnish 
to the Bank a request or requests for a deposit or deposits which do not exceed the 
aggregate amount of the Authorized Allocation. On the basis of such request or 
requests, the Bank shall, on behalf of the Borrower, withdraw from the Loan Account 
and deposit in the Special Accounts such amount or amounts as the Borrower shall have 
requested.
    (b)
(i)
For replenishment of the Special Accounts, the Borrower 
shall furnish to the Bank requests for deposits into the 
Special Accounts at such intervals as the Bank shall 
specify.
(ii)
Borrower shall furnish to the Bank the documents 
and other evidence required pursuant to paragraph 4 of this 
Schedule for the payment or payments in respect of which 
replenishment 
is requested. On the basis of each such 
request, the Bank shall, 
on behalf of the Borrower,  
withdraw from the Loan Account 
and deposit into the Special 
Accounts such amount as the Borrower 
shall have requested 
and as shall have been shown by said documents 
and other 
evidence to have been paid out of the Special Accounts for 
Eligible Expenditures.
All such deposits shall be withdrawn by the Bank from the Loan Account under the 
respective Eligible Categories, and in the respective equivalent amounts, as shall 
have been justified by said documents and other evidence.
4.
For each payment made by the Borrower out of the Special Accounts, the Borrower 
shall, at such time as the Bank shall reasonably request, furnish to the Bank such 
documents and other evidence showing that such payment was made exclusively for 
Eligible Expenditures.
5.
Notwithstanding the provisions of paragraph 3 of this Schedule, the Bank shall 
not be required to make further deposits into a
Special Account:
    (a)
If, at any time, the Bank shall have determined that all further 
withdrawals should be made by the Borrower directly from the Loan Account in 
accordance with the provisions of Article V of the General Conditions and paragraph 
(a) of Section 2.02 of this Agreement; or
    (b)
Once the total unwithdrawn amount of the Loan allocated to the Eligible 
Categories, less the amount of any outstanding special commitment entered into by the 
Bank pursuant to Section 5.02 of the General Conditions with respect to the Project, 
shall equal the equivalent of twice the amount of the Authorized Allocation.
Thereafter, withdrawal from the Loan Account of the remaining unwithdrawn amount of 
the Loan allocated to the Eligible Categories shall follow such procedures as the Bank 
shall specify by notice to the Borrower.  Such further withdrawals shall be made only 
after and to the extent that the Bank shall have been satisfied that all such amounts 
remaining on deposit in the said Special Account as of the date of such notice will be 
utilized in making payments for Eligible Expenditures.

(a)

payment out of a Special Account: (i) was made for an expenditure or in an amount not 
eligible pursuant to paragraph 2 of this Schedule; or (ii) was not justified by the

Bank: (A) provide such additional evidence as the Bank may request; or (B) deposit
